In 1978, to further streamline efforts to realize an independent state university, the Directorate General of Higher Education and the Regional Government of Central Sulawesi Province, formed the Central Sulawesi Higher Education (PTST) coordinator, chaired by the Governor of Central Sulawesi, with six deputy chairmen from from UNTAD UNHAS Branch (3 people) and IKIP Makassar Palu Branch (3 people). On August 14, 1981, the status of the second branch of the higher education institution was finally upgraded to a state tertiary institution, under the name Tadulako University (UNTAD) in accordance with a Presidential Decree. Based on the decision, UNTAD consists of 5 (five) faculties, namely: Faculty of Teacher Training and Education faculty of Social Science and Political Science faculty of Economics faculty of Law Faculty of Agriculture On October 21, 1993, a new faculty - the Faculty of Engineering - was established in accordance with the Minister of Education and Culture Decree
